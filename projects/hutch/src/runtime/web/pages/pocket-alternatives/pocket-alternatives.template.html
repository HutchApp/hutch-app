Why Hutch won't shut down

Pocket was acquired by Mozilla, then abandoned. Omnivore was acquired by ElevenLabs, then shut down overnight. The pattern is the same: a VC-funded company gets acquired and the product disappears.

Hutch has no venture capital. It's funded by a simple subscription: A$3.99/month after the founding member allocation fills up. That's the entire business model. No growth targets, no acquisition strategy, no board to answer to.

I'm Fayner Brack, the creator of js-cookie (22 billion+ annual CDN hits on jsDelivr). I've been shipping open source for over a decade. Hutch is AGPL source-available, so even if something happened to me, the code is public.

Your data is always exportable. Even if you cancel. I call it the "Even If You Cancel" promise.

How to switch from Pocket

  1. Export your Pocket data. Before the shutdown, Pocket offered a data export at getpocket.com/export. If you already exported your list as an HTML file, you're set. If you missed the window, check your email — Mozilla sent export reminders before July 8.
  2. Create a Hutch account. Sign up — it takes 30 seconds. The first 100 founding members get full access free, forever.
  3. Install the browser extension. Available for Firefox and Chrome. Save any page with one click, Ctrl/Cmd+D, or right-click.
  4. Import your Pocket articles (coming soon). I'm building a Pocket import tool that reads the standard Pocket HTML export file. It's not ready yet, but it's on the roadmap. For now, you can start fresh and save new articles immediately.
