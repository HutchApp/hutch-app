7 Best Pocket Alternatives in 2026

Pocket was acquired by Mozilla, then slowly abandoned. If you're looking for a read-it-later app that's still actively maintained, here are the best options — including the one I built after my own reading system outgrew Pocket years ago.

Try Hutch Free

Pocket was one of the first read-it-later apps. It worked well for years. Then Mozilla acquired it in 2017 and development slowed to a crawl. Omnivore launched as an open-source alternative, gained traction, then shut down overnight in November 2024 after being acquired by ElevenLabs.

The pattern is clear: read-it-later apps get acquired and abandoned. That's why data portability and long-term sustainability matter more than feature counts when choosing an alternative.

I've tested each of these apps and evaluated them on what actually matters for daily reading: save-and-forget simplicity, reading experience, and whether you can get your data out if the service disappears.

Why I built Hutch as a Pocket alternative

I maintained a personal reading system for 10 years — Gmail filters, DynamoDB tables, Reddit automations. When Pocket stagnated and Omnivore disappeared, I turned that system into a product anyone can use.

Hutch is built in Australia by a solo developer. It's open source, it includes an "Even If You Cancel" promise (your data is always exportable), and it's designed to be sustainable long-term rather than optimised for acquisition.

It doesn't have every feature yet. But everything that's shipped works, and I'm building the rest in public.
