What happened to Omnivore

On November 1, 2024, ElevenLabs acquired Omnivore. On November 15, the service shut down and data deletion began. Users had two weeks to export years of saved articles, highlights, and notes.

Omnivore was open source, community-loved, and had a clear mission. None of that mattered once the acquisition closed. The codebase went read-only. The API stopped responding. The newsletters stopped arriving.

The problem wasn't Omnivore's intentions. The problem was the business model. Venture-funded apps need an exit. When the exit comes, users are an afterthought.

Hutch has no investors. Revenue comes from subscriptions. There's no one to sell to and no reason to shut down. If Hutch ever stops working, the source code is AGPL-licensed and the export is always available.

Built by a developer who reads

I'm Fayner Brack. I created js-cookie, a JavaScript library with 22 billion+ downloads per year on jsDelivr. I've been building for the web for a long time.

For 10 years I maintained a personal reading pipeline — Gmail filters, DynamoDB tables, Reddit automations — that helped me save, organise, and actually read technical articles. That system generated 300,000+ Reddit karma across developer communities.

When Pocket was abandoned and Omnivore disappeared, I turned that personal system into Hutch. One developer, building in public, one feature at a time.

FAQ

Common questions from Omnivore users

What happened to Omnivore?

ElevenLabs acquired Omnivore on November 1, 2024, and shut it down on November 15. Users had roughly two weeks to export their data before deletion began. The open-source repository was archived. The team moved to ElevenLabs to work on text-to-speech, not reading tools. Omnivore is not coming back.

Is there a free Omnivore alternative?

Hutch is free for the first 100 founding members — full access, forever. After that, it's A$3.99/month (roughly US$2.60). Self-hosted alternatives like Karakeep and Wallabag are free but require you to run your own server. Readwise Reader is the most feature-complete alternative at $12.99/month.

Can I import my Omnivore data into Hutch?

Not yet. Omnivore export import is on the roadmap. If you exported your data before the shutdown, hold onto that file. In the meantime, you can start fresh with the browser extension — save any article with one click.
