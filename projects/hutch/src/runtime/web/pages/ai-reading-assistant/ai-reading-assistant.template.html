Hutch: An AI Reading Assistant That Helps You Read More, Not Less.

Most AI reading tools try to replace reading with summaries, digests, and auto-curation. Hutch uses AI to help you read more of the right things.

The Problem

What an AI reading assistant should actually be.

The phrase "AI reading assistant" has been co-opted by tools that want to read for you. They generate summaries of articles you never open. They send digest emails of content an algorithm picked. They auto-curate feeds based on what's trending, not what matters to you.

That's not assistance. That's replacement.

A real AI reading assistant should reduce the friction in your reading workflow. It should help you decide what to read faster, so you spend more time actually reading. The goal isn't fewer articles in less time. It's more of the right articles, read properly.

The Approach

How Hutch uses AI.

Hutch generates a TL;DR summary for every article you save. Not a replacement for reading the article — a tool for deciding whether to read it now, later, or not at all.

That's it. No auto-curated feeds. No digest emails. No "here's what you should read today" notifications. No algorithmic ranking that quietly decides what you see and what you don't.

You save what catches your attention. Hutch summarises it so you can triage faster. When something looks worth your time, you open it and read the real thing in a clean, distraction-free reader view.

The AI stays in the background. You stay in control.

The Difference

Hutch doesn't decide what you read. You decide. AI helps you decide faster.

Most AI reading tools sit between you and your content. They filter, rank, and curate — and over time, you stop choosing what to read. The algorithm chooses for you. Your reading diet narrows to whatever the model thinks you want.

Hutch works differently. Every article in your list is one you saved yourself. The AI summary helps you scan your own list faster, but it never removes, hides, or reorders anything. Your reading list is yours.

This is a deliberate design choice. An AI reading assistant that decides what you should read is just another feed algorithm. And you already have enough of those.

The Loop

Save. Summarise. Triage. Read.

1

Save

Hit the browser extension on any page. One click, keyboard shortcut, or right-click. The article is saved to your reading list.

2

Summarise

Hutch automatically generates a TL;DR for every saved article. The summary highlights the key points so you know what the article covers before you open it.

3

Triage

Scan your list by reading the TL;DRs. Decide what to read now, what to save for later, and what to skip. No algorithm involved — just you, making faster decisions.

4

Read

Open the article in a clean reader view. No ads, no pop-ups, no cookie banners. Just the content, rendered for comfortable reading. Dark mode included.
