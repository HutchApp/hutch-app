Your reading list is a graveyard. You saved 40 articles last week. You read three. The rest sit there, getting older, making you feel bad every time you scroll past them.

The problem is not saving. The problem is choosing.

## AI TL;DR for every article

Hutch generates a 2-3 sentence summary for every article you save. No extra clicks. No waiting. The summary shows up in your reading list, right next to the title.

Scan ten articles in thirty seconds. Open the ones that matter. Skip the rest.

I built this because I spent a decade curating reading lists that grew faster than I could read them. The TL;DR turns a guilt pile into a menu. You see what each article is about and you pick.

## Then actually read it

When something is worth your time, open it in reader view. Hutch strips the page down to the article text. No ads, no popups, no sticky nav bars, no cookie banners.

Dark mode follows your system preference. The text is clean. The spacing is generous. The layout stays at a comfortable reading width.

This is the part that matters. Sitting with an article and reading it. Not skimming a summary and pretending you learned something.

## Not another AI slop machine

I know what you are thinking. Another app that replaces reading with AI-generated mush.

That is not what this is. The summary helps you *choose* what to read. You still read the article. The original words, by the original author. That is the point.

Hutch does not rewrite anything. It does not generate content. It gives you a two-sentence preview so you can decide where to spend your attention. AI is the filter, not the replacement.
