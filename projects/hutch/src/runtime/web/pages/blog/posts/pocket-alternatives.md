Mozilla shut down Pocket on July 8, 2025. Millions of reading lists vanished overnight. If you are looking for a replacement, this post covers what I built and why.

Hutch is a read-it-later app I made from a personal system I ran for 10 years. Save articles in one click, read them in a clean reader view, and get a TL;DR for each one. Your data is yours to export at any time.

## What works today

Everything listed here is shipped and working. No waitlists.

**Browser extension.** Save any page in one click from Chrome or Firefox. Works with Ctrl/Cmd+D and right-click too.

**Reader view.** A clean reading mode powered by Readability.js. Strips ads, sidebars, and popups.

**AI TL;DR summaries.** Each saved article gets a short summary of the key points. Cached per URL. Included in the subscription at no extra cost.

**Dark mode.** Matches your system setting automatically.

**Full data export.** Download all your saved articles at any time. This works after cancellation too.

**Privacy-first hosting.** Hosted in Sydney, Australia. Compliant with the Australian Privacy Act. Outside US jurisdiction. No tracking, no ads.

## What's coming

These are planned, not promised. I ship one feature at a time and I build in public.

- **Tags and organisation.** Organise saved articles into categories.
- **Full-text search.** Search across all your saved articles.
- **Highlights and notes.** Mark passages and add notes as you read.
- **Pocket import tool.** Upload your Pocket HTML export file into Hutch.

## Why Hutch won't shut down

Pocket was acquired by Mozilla, then abandoned. Omnivore was acquired by ElevenLabs, then shut down with one week's notice. The pattern repeats: a company takes venture capital, gets acquired, and the product disappears.

Hutch has no venture capital. It runs on a subscription: A$3.99/month after the first 100 founding members. That is the entire business model. No growth targets, no acquisition plan, no board.

I'm [Fagner Brack](https://www.linkedin.com/in/fagnerbrack/), the creator of [js-cookie](https://www.jsdelivr.com/package/npm/js-cookie) (22 billion+ annual CDN hits on jsDelivr). I have shipped open source software for over 10 years. Hutch is AGPL source-available. The code is public.

You can export your data at any time. Cancel your account and your articles are still there to download. I call this the "Even If You Cancel" promise.

## How to switch from Pocket

1. **Export your Pocket data.** Pocket had a data export at getpocket.com/export. Your export is an HTML file. Check your email for Mozilla's export reminders if you missed the window.
2. **Create a Hutch account.** [Sign up here](https://hutch-app.com/signup). It takes 30 seconds. The first 100 founding members get full access free, forever.
3. **Install the browser extension.** Available for [Firefox](https://hutch-app.com/install?browser=firefox) and [Chrome](https://hutch-app.com/install?browser=chrome). Save any page with one click, Ctrl/Cmd+D, or right-click.
4. **Import your Pocket articles (planned).** I am building a Pocket import tool for the standard HTML export file. It is not ready yet. You can start fresh and save new articles right away.
