Save anything technical

The browser extension saves any page in one click. Blog posts explaining a tricky algorithm. GitHub READMEs you want to revisit. Stack Overflow answers that actually solved your problem. RFC documents. Conference talk transcripts. HN threads with 400 comments where the real insight is buried at comment #47.

Ctrl/Cmd+D or right-click. The page is saved, content extracted, and available in your reading queue. No copy-pasting URLs into yet another app.

AI TL;DR for triage

You saved 30 articles this week. You have time to deep-read maybe 5. The TL;DR summary for each article lets you scan your queue and decide what is worth your time — and what can wait or be discarded.

Powered by DeepSeek. Summaries are generated automatically when you save an article. Included in the price, not gated behind an upsell tier.

Reader view

Clean reading without cookie consent banners, newsletter popups, sticky nav bars, and sidebar ads. Just the article content with good typography.

Dark mode follows your system preference. Built on Mozilla's Readability engine — the same one behind Firefox Reader View. Good for late-night reading sessions when you finally get to that article about distributed consensus you saved three weeks ago.

Built in the open

  • AGPL source-available. The full codebase is on GitHub. Read the code that handles your data.
  • Australian Privacy Act. Hosted in Sydney. Your data is not subject to US jurisdiction or the CLOUD Act.
  • Full data export. JSON export of everything, anytime. No lock-in. If you leave, your data leaves with you.
  • No VC funding. No growth targets. No pivot pressure. No "acqui-hire" exit that shuts down the product. Built to last, not to fundraise.
