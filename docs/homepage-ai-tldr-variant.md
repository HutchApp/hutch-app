## AI TL;DR for every article

Save a blog post, a news article, or a 6,000-word investigation. Hutch generates a 2-3 sentence summary for each one. No prompt engineering. No chatbot. Just a TL;DR that tells you what the piece says.

Skim your reading list in seconds. Open only what earns your time.

Here is an example. You save a 4,000-word article about battery chemistry. The TL;DR reads: "Solid-state batteries won't reach consumer vehicles before 2030. Lithium-ion improvements are outpacing them. The hype cycle is driven by investor pressure, not engineering milestones."

Now you know if you want to read it. That took five seconds.

---

## Then actually read it

Hutch gives you a clean reader view. Article text, good typography, nothing else. Dark mode follows your system setting.

No algorithmic feed. No recommendations competing for your attention. Just the article you picked, presented for reading.

This is a reading app. It should feel like reading.

---

## Not another AI slop machine

You are probably thinking: "Great, another app that replaces reading with AI."

No. The summary helps you *choose*. You still read the article. That is the point.

The web is full of AI-generated filler. Listicles that say nothing. Summaries of summaries. Hutch uses AI for one job: helping you decide what deserves your time. Then it steps aside.

The TL;DR is a filter, not a replacement.
