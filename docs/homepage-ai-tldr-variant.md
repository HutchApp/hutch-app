## AI TL;DR for every article

Save anything — blog post, news article, long-form investigation — and Hutch generates a 2–3 sentence summary automatically. No prompt engineering. No chatbot. Just a TL;DR that tells you what the piece actually says.

Skim your reading list in seconds. Open only what's worth your time.

Now you know if you want to read it. That took five seconds.

## Then actually read it

When something earns your attention, Hutch gives you a clean reader view — article text, good typography, nothing else. Dark mode follows your system automatically.

## Not another AI slop machine

I know what you're thinking. "Great, another app that replaces reading with AI summaries."

No. The summary helps you *choose*. You still read the article. That's the point.

The web is drowning in AI-generated content — listicles that say nothing, summaries of summaries, engagement-optimised filler. Hutch uses AI for one thing: helping you decide what deserves your actual attention. Then it gets out of the way.

The TL;DR is a filter, not a replacement.
